<p><p>The acid beverages market is categorized into low acid and high acid segments based on pH levels. Low acid beverages, such as certain fruit juices and dairy drinks, typically have a pH above 4.6 and are prone to microbial growth, requiring specific preservation techniques. Conversely, high acid beverages, like citrus juices and carbonated drinks, have a pH below 4.6, which inhibits bacterial growth, allowing for more straightforward shelf-stability. Each type targets different consumer preferences and food safety standards.</p></p>

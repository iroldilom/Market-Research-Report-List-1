<p><p>The photoconductive cell market primarily comprises two types: Cadmium Sulphide (CdS) and Cadmium Selenide (CdSe) cells. CdS cells are widely used in light-sensitive applications such as photoresistors and light meters due to their sensitivity to visible light. In contrast, CdSe cells are favored for their efficiency in infrared applications and as photodetectors in various optoelectronic devices. Both materials leverage their semiconductor properties to convert light energy into electrical signals, catering to diverse industries such as telecommunications and security.</p></p>

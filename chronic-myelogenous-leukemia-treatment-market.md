<p><p>The Chronic Myelogenous Leukemia (CML) treatment market is primarily divided into two categories: disease-specific treatment and symptomatic treatment. Disease-specific treatment focuses on targeting the underlying pathology of CML, often involving tyrosine kinase inhibitors that selectively inhibit abnormal cell growth. In contrast, symptomatic treatment addresses the symptoms and complications associated with CML, enhancing patient comfort and quality of life. Both approaches are essential in managing the disease, with disease-specific treatments aiming for long-term remission, while symptomatic treatments provide supportive care.</p></p>

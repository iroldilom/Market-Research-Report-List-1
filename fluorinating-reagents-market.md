<p><ul><li>Electrophilic Fluorinating Reagents</li><li>Nucleophilic Fluorinating Reagents</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The fluorinating reagents market is primarily classified into two types: electrophilic and nucleophilic fluorinating reagents. Electrophilic fluorinating reagents are compounds that introduce fluorine by acting as electrophiles, usually promoting the substitution of other atoms or groups in organic molecules. In contrast, nucleophilic fluorinating reagents donate fluoride ions, typically engaging in nucleophilic substitution reactions. These reagents are vital in pharmaceuticals, agrochemicals, and materials science, enabling the synthesis of fluorinated compounds with enhanced properties.</p></p>

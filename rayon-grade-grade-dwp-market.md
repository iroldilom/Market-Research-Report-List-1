<p><p>Rayon grade DWP (Dissolving Wood Pulp) is primarily used in the production of rayon fibers and other derivatives. The market for DWP consists of two main types: alkali wood pulp (sulfate process) and sulfite wood pulp. Alkali wood pulp is produced through the kraft process, yielding higher strength and purity, making it suitable for high-quality rayon. In contrast, sulfite wood pulp, produced through the sulfite process, offers lower strength but is still used in specific rayon applications. Together, they support the textile and nonwoven industries' demand for sustainable fiber sources.</p></p>

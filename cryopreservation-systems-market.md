<p><p>Cryopreservation systems encompass various types tailored for specific temperature ranges crucial for preserving biological samples. Blood refrigerators maintain temperatures at 4℃±1℃, ideal for storing blood products. Medical refrigerators operate between 2℃ and 8℃ for pharmaceuticals. Biomedical freezers function from -10℃ to -50℃, suitable for sensitive biological materials. Ultra-low freezers, ranging from -50℃ to -150℃, are designed for long-term storage of cells and tissues. Liquid nitrogen tanks, maintaining temperatures of -150℃ to -196℃, are used for cryogenic preservation. Other systems may include specialized storage units for distinct applications.</p></p>

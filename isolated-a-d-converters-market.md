<p><p>The isolated A-D converters market features three primary interface types: CMOS, LVDS, and Parallel. CMOS interface converters are known for low power consumption and ease of integration, making them ideal for compact applications. LVDS interface converters offer high-speed data transmission with reduced electromagnetic interference, suitable for high-performance systems. Meanwhile, parallel interface converters enable simultaneous data transfer across multiple channels, which enhances speed but may require more space. Each type caters to specific application needs in isolation and data conversion.</p></p>

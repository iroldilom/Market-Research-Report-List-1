<p><p>Expression vectors are tools used to introduce foreign genes into host cells for protein production. Bacterial expression systems utilize simple prokaryotic cells for rapid and cost-effective protein synthesis, ideal for uncomplicated proteins. Mammalian vectors offer a more complex environment, enabling post-translational modifications for eukaryotic proteins, crucial for therapeutic applications. Yeast systems combine efficiency and eukaryotic features, making them suitable for certain recombinant proteins. Insect cell systems leverage advantages of both eukaryotes and robust expression, particularly for larger proteins.</p></p>

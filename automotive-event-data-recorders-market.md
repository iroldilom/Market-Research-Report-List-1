<p><p>Automotive Event Data Recorders (EDRs) are classified into two main market types: Type I and Type II. Type I EDRs primarily capture basic crash data, including vehicle speed, brake engagement, and airbag deployment at the time of an accident. In contrast, Type II EDRs offer more advanced capabilities, recording additional parameters such as pre-crash vehicle dynamics and driver behavior. This enhanced data aids in detailed accident analysis and improves vehicle safety features, making them invaluable for both manufacturers and insurers.</p></p>
